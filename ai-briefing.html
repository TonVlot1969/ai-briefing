Good morning Ton, here is your Monday AI briefing. This week the big theme is that your data tools are quietly turning into agents, and the consulting world is starting to sweat about what that means for billable hours.

Start with the practical stuff for your teams. Databricks shipped a wave of updates to Genie this month. The headline one is Genie Ontology, which automatically builds a map of your business by reading your existing dashboards, notebooks and pipelines, so the answers get sharper without anyone hand feeding it context. Even better for your BI people, you can now drop a Tableau or Power BI file straight into Genie and it rebuilds the dashboard for you. Microsoft also pushed its June Power BI and Fabric update, including Fabric Apps, an AI first way to build data apps straight on top of your semantic models. Both of these mean less plumbing, more answering questions.

Now the delivery and consulting angle, which is right in your lane. A fresh 2026 consulting review made a sharp point. AI already makes consultants far more productive, but firms are slow to deploy it fully because it shrinks the billable hours they sell. That is exactly the tension your fixed price AI delivery model sidesteps. When you own the outcome, not the hours, productivity becomes your margin, not your enemy. Worth saying out loud to your teams.

For something a colleague can actually demo, Alteryx just previewed Agent Studio, which turns an existing data workflow into an autonomous agent without writing code. Very visual, very on theme for the session.

My recommendation. Demo the Databricks Genie trick of importing a Power BI file and then chatting with it in plain language. It is live, it is visual, and every data consultant in the room will instantly see the time it saves. Have a good week.
