Good morning Ton, here is your Monday AI briefing. This week was all about data tools getting smarter, with Databricks and Power BI both putting an assistant right where your consultants already work.

Start with the practical stuff for the teams. At the Databricks Data and AI Summit this month, Genie went native inside Microsoft Teams, Copilot, and Excel. The Excel add-in is now in public preview, so a Business Analyst can pull live lakehouse data into a spreadsheet with no SQL and no ODBC setup, and even write changes back. In the same window, Microsoft shipped a June update to Power BI where Copilot can build a semantic model from plain language, renaming tables, creating relationships, and generating DAX measures for you. And Google's Looker added an Expression Assistant that writes measures and filters from a description. For BI Consultants and Data Engineers, this is the boring work getting automated, which is exactly what we want.

On delivery and consulting, there's a useful reality check. A widely shared 2026 update on consulting and AI made the point that AI makes consultants far more productive, but firms hesitate to fully deploy it because it shrinks billable hours. The way out is outcome based and fixed price delivery, which is precisely the bet you're making at ASML. Worth a slide.

For something to show, the model side stayed busy too, with Anthropic's Fable 5 landing on the ninth and Gemini 3.5 Pro expected any day.

My recommendation for this week's session: demo the Databricks Genie Excel add-in. It's visual, it needs no code, and everyone in the room lives in Excel. Show a question typed in plain English returning live data, then a write back. It makes the AI-in-data story concrete in two minutes. Have a good week.
