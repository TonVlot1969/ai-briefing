Good morning Ton, here is your Monday AI briefing. This was the week the big data platforms stopped talking about agents and started shipping them, and one consultancy proved it saves real hours.

Start with the tools your teams actually touch. At its Data and AI Summit last week, Databricks launched Genie One, an agentic coworker that answers questions across structured and unstructured data in plain language, and it expanded Agent Bricks so developers can build agents with the Claude and OpenAI SDKs right on top of the Lakehouse. Snowflake countered with big upgrades to CoCo, its data-native coding agent that turns a plain English prompt into production SQL, dbt models and Airflow pipelines, and it now hits a 72 percent pass rate on real analytics engineering tasks. Power BI's June update leans the same way, with agent skills and Copilot replacing hand-written DAX. For your Data Engineers, BI Consultants and Business Analysts, this is the direction of travel. Less typing, more reviewing.

Now the part that matters for delivery. Cognizant reported that scaling Snowflake CoCo freed up 1,300 hours and cut costs across its teams. That is exactly the proof point you need for the fixed-price model. Real AI end to end lowering the resource bill while keeping quality. EY also launched an AI-native software delivery approach that aims to go from idea to production in days. The honest caveat, CIOs note that individual productivity gains often do not show up in company throughput yet, so measurement matters.

My recommendation for this week's session, demo Snowflake CoCo or Databricks Genie turning a plain English question into working SQL. It is visual, it lands in two minutes, and it shows your consultants the shift from writing code to reviewing it. Have a good week.
