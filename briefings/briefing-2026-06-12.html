Good morning Ton, here is your Monday AI briefing. This week the big theme is agents moving right into the data stack, with Microsoft, Snowflake and the big consultancies all shipping tools your teams can actually use.

Start with practical data work. Microsoft's June Power BI update is a real one for your BI people. Copilot in web modeling is now in preview, so you can describe a semantic model in plain English and it renames tables, builds relationships and writes DAX measures for you. There are also new report authoring agent skills that let someone design, validate and publish a report just by talking to it, no manual file editing. For BI Consultants and Business Analysts this is exactly the kind of grunt work that eats their days.

On the warehouse side, Snowflake pushed Cortex Analyst into public preview, an agentic system that turns plain questions into accurate SQL. They also added a Claude Code plugin, so engineers can drive their data stack from inside Claude. Databricks holds its Data and AI Summit mid June, so expect more agentic announcements within days.

Now delivery and consulting, which is closest to your world. EY launched an AI native delivery model that claims to move from idea to production in days instead of months, and Microsoft's Work IQ API goes generally available on the sixteenth, letting you build agents on top of your own work data. This is the fixed price story you keep pushing, proof that end to end AI lowers the resource bill while raising output.

For demo material there is plenty to choose from. My recommendation for this week's session is Power BI Copilot in web modeling. It is hands on, your team lives in Power BI, and watching it build DAX from a single sentence lands the point better than any slide. End there and let someone try it live. Have a good week.
